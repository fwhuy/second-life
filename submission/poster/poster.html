A six-slot classifier must label anything — even a cat. Our deployment adds a feature-space guard that flags out-of-domain photos instead of forcing a wrong class — the refusal that matters at a real bin.

Takeaways

  • Two strong variations — a cap-fitting CNN and a high-capacity transformer, both in the high-90s.
  • Split by object, not image, so a score reflects the material, not a memorised photo.
  • Deploy with an open-set guard: a classifier should know when a photo is not one of its classes.
  • Offline Flask app · both models on one photo · bilingual zh / en
